Home Remedies for Lung Diseases


1. Black Tea

Respiratory diseases get smoldered by this drink as it clears your duct to enhance passage and exit of fresh air. Drink this tea every morning and twice later on to get the boost it provides.

2. Citrus Fruits

The family of citrus fruits is richly endowed with vitamins which are effective in respiratory tract repairs and elimination of impediments on your respiratory tract.

3. Exercise

Exercise helps to keep the body in an optimal state with a better circulation of blood and supply of oxygen. Indulging in regular aerobics will expedite respiratory efficiency.

4. Garlic

The following garlic remedy will facilitate clear congestion in your lungs throughout the first stages of the respiratory disease. Boil two or three cloves in a one-quarter cup of milk. Leave this mix it to chill, and then drink it.

5. Cold Food Avoidance

Cold foods will lead to more congestion in the respiratory tracts when the processed air is trapped therein and not offloaded. It is better to avoid these foods when winter sets in so that your respiratory efficiency can be enhanced.

6. Avoid Fumes

Keep off areas where there is likely to be fumes or smoke. Since respiratory difficulties are propagated with the presence of smoke, it only comes out as a wise counsel to avoid these triggers.

7. Good Hygiene

Maintaining good hygiene keeps many infections at bay, and the easily overlooked tip is thorough hand washing. Use ordinary soap to wash your hands clean when you are back indoors and before you eat.

8. Camphors

Camphor is used as an effective remedy for many ailments but it works well as one of the home remedies for Atelectasis. The challenge to meet is avoiding adulterated products which are now rampant.

9. Cinnamon and Honey

These two have properties that work well against respiratory infections and since they are easily added to warm water, they can be mixed and taken with tea or walk milk. A daily routine of this will do you lots of good.

10. Ginger

Ginger is widely reviewed as a powerful anti-inflammatory, antibiotic and warming remedy for a number of health infractions. When infections lead to excessive production of mucus, ginger can clear it up and remedy the situation.
